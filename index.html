About Me

About Me

I'm currently a third year English and Professional Writing student at York University. This website functions as a portfolio for work I completed throughout the Winter 2020/2021 course, WRIT3001 Intermediate Digital Authoring. The first project was a sonic essay, where I speak about my experiences in digital authoring using audio cues. The second project was a video essay, where I explored the same theme by using visual cues, illustrating how I have used different tools in my digital authoring practices.

Sonic Essay

For this project, I narrated my earliest experiences in digital authoring, and used musical interludes to signify both mood and changes of topic. It was a chance to explore how text and sound can be used to tell a story.


Sonic Essay Transcript

(00:00 – Music introduction, “Lahaina” from Blue Dot Sessions)

(00:10)
Hi, my name is Kelsey and today I will be talking about some of my earliest experiences in digital authoring to the more recent ones and what I’ve learned from them.


(00:18 Music interlude 1)


(00:25)
The first social media account I ever made was my YouTube account, which I made a long time ago. Because of this, I’ve seen YouTube change a lot throughout the years and have come to see how it has become more restrictive, while at the same time becoming more popular, and consisting of a variety of different forms of entertainment.

(00:41 Music interlude 2, “Bossa Boa” from Blue Dot Sessions)

(00:43)
From what I can remember, YouTube was originally created as a website to watch videos and TV. People were able to watch entire anime series, as well as music videos uploaded by fans that were not met with copyright restrictions. I even once uploaded an MTV special of a band I liked, only to have the video removed months later as it violated copyright.

(1:03 “Glass Smashing Bottles” from Freesound.org)

(1:05)
A lot of the changes came after YouTube was bought by Google. When I tried to upload videos of a concert I went to, I was eventually met with the same restrictions, and while it was fun to interact with other fans, all good things must come to an end.

(1:18 Music interlude 3, “Trois Gnossiennes 3” from Blue Dot Sessions) (1:25)
Around this time, Blogger was also very popular and I had been following a lot of different blogs, from personal diaries to tutorials on graphic design. I remember having Photoshop randomly installed on our computer and just making a lot of random graphics like wallpapers, fake posters for bands that didn’t exist, pretty much anything. I learned a little bit about web design, like HTML, CSS, and I decided I’d try to make my own blog, designing the theme and setting up the layout.

(1:50 Music interlude 3, “Trois Gnossiennes 3”)

(1:58)
After this I created a fan-centred blog on Tumblr, where I once again spent a lot of time creating and editing the theme and other graphics, and where I would interact with fans by sharing our writing. Later, while in university I took the course Writing in Digital Cultures, where we had to create a WordPress blog, which focused on my musical interests, and for which I designed the logo and layout.

(2:19 Music interlude 4, “Throughput” from Blue Dot Sessions)

(2:26)
From all these experiences in digital authoring, I’ve come to realize that my interests in digital authorship revolve mainly around design and learning how to create something that looks good. While I do enjoy writing, I also like to balance it with creating graphics, and learning how to better present my own writing. At the moment, I’m learning how to use Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop so that I can better express my interests in new and interesting ways.

(2:50 Music interlude, “Throughput”)

(3:02)
The End

Video Essay

In the video essay, I wanted to provide a visual representation of what my earliest digital authoring experiences looked like, to what they look like now. For this I used both screenshots of old blogging platforms and social media websites, along with video of me using an old version of Adobe Photoshop. The video then ends with me using Adobe InDesign CC, where a lot of my focus is now.
